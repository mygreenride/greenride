San Diego Information

San Diego is California's second largest city and the United States' eighth largest, encompassing numerous other charming neighborhoods and communities, including downtown's historic Gaslamp Quarter, Little Italy, Coronado, La Jolla, Del Mar, Carlsbad, Escondido, La Mesa, Hillcrest, Barrio Logan, Chula Vista.

San Diego is renowned for its idyllic climate, 70 miles of pristine beaches and a dazzling array of world-class family attractions. Popular attractions include the world-famous San Diego Zoo and Wild Animal Park, Sea World San Diego and LEGOLAND California. San Diego’s Balboa Park, the largest urban cultural park in the U.S., is home to over 15 museums, numerous art galleries, beautiful gardens, the Tony Award-winning The Globe Theatres and the San Diego Zoo.

San Diego County also features over 90 golf courses and a variety of exciting sports, beachfront resorts and luxury spas, gaming, a dynamic downtown district, annual special events and unique holiday offerings, multicultural festivals and celebrations, colorful neighborhoods and communities, a rich military history and much more.

For more information on San Diego visit San Diego Convention and Visitors Bureau

San Diego International Airport (SAN) Information

Founded in 1928, San Diego International Airport is the 30th busiest airport in the country in terms of passengers and the busiest single-runway commercial service airport in the nation.

  1. The Airport is conveniently located three miles northwest of downtown San Diego and sits on 661 acres.
  2. The Airport's passenger and cargo airlines operate close to 300 departures daily.
  3. The Airport and affiliated enterprises contribute an estimated $9.9 billion annually to the regional economy.
  4. An improvement program investing more than $232 million was completed on NOVEMBER 7, 1998. Projects included a 300,000-square-foot, eight-gate expansion of Terminal 2, a new roadway system, complete remodeling of the existing terminal interiors, new shops and restaurants, increased parking and a new baggage claim area.
  5. Approximately 4,900 employees work for the San Diego County Regional Airport Authority, airlines, support service providers, federal agencies, fixed base operator, cargo operators and concessions at the Airport.
  6. In 1976, San Diego International Airport became the first commercial airport in the nation to enact restrictions on late night and early morning takeoffs. Aircraft departures are not allowed between 11:30 p.m. and 6:30 a.m.
